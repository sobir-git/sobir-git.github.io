Some statistical terms

Sat 01 May 2021
By Sobir Bobiev
Category: probability and statistics

Joint probability distribution function

It characterizes the probability of a random vector in its domain. So, for a random vector \(X \in R^n\), it is a function

$$f_X: R^n \to [0, \inf) .$$

Computing the integral over the support region should result in \(1\).

There is another term called joint distribution function which is a synonym to joint cumulative distribution function (also joint cdf). At a given point \(x\) it gives the probability that all components of \(X\) are smaller than the corresponding component \(x\).

Marginal distribution function

For a random vector, it is the probability of one of its components. So, for a random vector \(X \in R^n\) and its component \(X_i\), it is a function

$$f_{X_i}: R \to [0, \inf) .$$
